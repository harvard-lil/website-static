Summer 2022 Research Assistants

About us

The Library Innovation Lab is a team of librarians, programmers, artists, and lawyers with a broad mission: to build open source tools and services, open to everyone and in the public interest, solving serious emerging problems from the unique perspective of the world's largest academic law library. On the tech side we work most often with Django, Vue, and Postgres.

Summer 2022 Research Assistants

Deadline extended: we will be accepting applications for this role on a rolling basis through May 1, 2022.

We're seeking incoming first-year law students with a background in technology or design to join the Library Innovation Lab as summer research assistants. Join us this summer to get a head start on adapting to law school and hack on sites that expand access to the law.

We work at the intersection of libraries, technology, and the law, building and supporting projects that broaden access to information through open source software. This summer, we're seeking research assistants to participate broadly in the life and work of LIL, and to help our team advance our development goals in the fields of open access data and learning.

As a research assistant, you will have the opportunity to jump into legal academia via a technical standpoint, rooting yourself in a law library focused both on innovation and legal tradition. You will help us imagine and act on new ways to serve the growing community of faculty, librarians and students who are interested in our three tent-pole projects: H2O Open Casebook, The Caselaw Access Project and Perma.cc.

Our team will work with each research assistant to scope out a development roadmap that builds upon our current project goals and highlights the candidate's interests. We're looking for a collaborator, not an intern.

The ideal research assistant will be an incoming law student with a technical background who is curious, self-motivated and eager to interact directly with our team and the broader law school community. While neither legal nor specific technical expertise is required, we are seeking candidates who would feel comfortable developing alongside a team who works mainly with Django and Vue.

To take a look at our code and get a sense of work, please visit https://github.com/harvard-lil.

Location and hours

35 hours per week, in person or remote within the United States.

Eligibility

Applicants must be eligible to work within the United States. While the program is tailored to incoming law students from any law school, we are happy to consider applicants from other backgrounds as well, with cover letter explaining interest.

Compensation

$20/hour

Dates
